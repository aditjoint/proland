📋 Included Legal Provisions (50+ Clauses)

This agreement incorporates the following legally binding provisions:

  • No Tenancy Creation: The arrangement creates a License only under Indian Easements Act 1882. The occupant acquires no tenancy rights, leasehold interest, or statutory protection under any Rent Control Act.
  • Ownership & Possession: The licensor retains absolute legal title, possession, and control of the premises. The licensee receives only a revocable right of occupation.
  • Residential Use Covenant: The premises shall be used exclusively for residential purposes by the declared occupants only. Any commercial, business, or income-generating use is prohibited.
  • Restricted Occupancy: Only the listed occupants are permitted to reside. No additional persons may occupy without prior written consent. Unauthorized occupation constitutes material breach.
  • Identity Documentation: All occupants' names, ages, identification proof, and relationship are declared and fixed. No substitution or change is permitted during the term.
  • Guest Policy & Duration: Short-term overnight guests are permitted for maximum 7 consecutive days. Extended visits require written permission.
  • Nuisance & Disturbance: Any noise, music, gathering, or activity causing disturbance to neighbors or society constitutes immediate breach. The licensor may terminate summarily.
  • Criminal Activity Clause: If any occupant is subject to criminal complaint, FIR filing, arrest, or police investigation, the agreement shall terminate immediately without notice.
  • Non-Compliance With Society Rules: Violation of society/community bylaws constitutes breach. The licensor may terminate if occupant receives complaints from authorities or management.
  • Payment Obligations: The monthly license fee is due by the specified date. Late payment beyond 3 days incurs 2% monthly penalty plus 18% annual interest on overdue amount.
  • Property Damage Liability: The occupant is liable for all damage beyond normal wear and tear. Willful damage results in forfeiture of 3x deposit amount plus repair costs.
  • Security Deposit Terms: The deposit is held in trust. It is refundable subject to no damage, unpaid fees, or violations. Full or partial forfeiture applies for breaches.
  • Utilities & Consumption: All electricity, water, gas, internet, and other consumption-based charges are the occupant's responsibility. Bills must be paid timely.
  • Inspection & Access Rights: The licensor retains unrestricted right to enter for inspection, repairs, or viewing by prospective licensees, with 24 hours' notice (except emergencies).
  • Lien on Occupant's Property: The licensor has paramount lien on all goods and chattels within the premises for unpaid fees and dues. Items may be seized after 15 days' notice.
  • Notice of Termination: The licensor may terminate with immediate effect for material breach. Occupant must vacate within 24 hours of termination notice.
  • Holdover Charges: If occupant remains after termination, a penalty of ₹2,000 per day applies for each day of unauthorized occupation.
  • Vacant Handover: Upon termination or expiry, occupant must deliver vacant, clean possession. All keys must be returned. Professional cleaning certification may be required.
  • Sub-letting & Transfer Prohibition: The occupant may not sub-let, assign, or transfer the license. Any attempt is grounds for immediate termination.
  • Prohibition on Statutory Registration: The occupant confirms no GST, professional tax, MSME, or commercial registration shall be obtained against the premises address.
  • Indemnity for Statutory Violations: The occupant indemnifies the licensor against all GST demands, tax notices, municipal penalties, or statutory action arising from occupant's misuse.
  • Insurance & Liability: The occupant maintains responsibility for contents insurance. The licensor is not liable for theft, loss, or damage to occupant's belongings.
  • Society & Regulatory Compliance: The occupant shall comply with all society bylaws, municipal rules, and applicable building regulations. Violations are grounds for termination.
